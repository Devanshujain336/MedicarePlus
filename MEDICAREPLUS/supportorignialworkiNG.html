Indian Government Health Guidelines & Financial Support Programs

Maternal & Child Health Programs

1. Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY)

Benefit: Cash incentive of ₹5,000 in three installments for first living child.
Eligibility: Pregnant women and lactating mothers, excluding those who receive similar benefits under other schemes.
Process: First installment (₹1,000) at early registration, second (₹2,000) at 6 months with at least 1 ANC, third (₹2,000) after childbirth registration and first vaccination.
Application: Apply at Anganwadi centers or health facilities with Aadhaar card, bank account, and ID proof.
More Info:https://pmmvy.wcd.gov.in/

2. Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY)

Benefit: Cash assistance for institutional delivery; ₹1,400 in rural and ₹1,000 in urban areas for low-performing states; ₹700 in rural and ₹600 in urban areas for high-performing states.
Eligibility: All pregnant women in low-performing states; BPL, SC/ST women in high-performing states.
Components: Free delivery, free C-section, free drugs and consumables, free diagnostics, free diet during stay, free transport.
Application: Register at nearest health center with ID proof, BPL card (if applicable), and bank account details.
More Info:https://www.nhm.gov.in/index1.php?lang=1&level=3&sublinkid=841&lid=309

3. Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (JSSK)

Benefit: Entitles pregnant women to absolutely free delivery including C-section in public health institutions.
Eligibility: All pregnant women and sick newborns accessing public health institutions.
Services: Free drugs, consumables, diagnostics, blood, diet, transport to and from facility, exemption from all user charges.
Application: No formal application; services provided at all government hospitals.
More Info:https://www.nhm.gov.in/index4.php?lang=1&level=0&linkid=150&lid=171

4. LaQshya (Labour Room Quality Improvement Initiative)

Objective: Improve quality of care in labour rooms and maternity operation theatres.
Facilities Covered: All Medical College Hospitals, District Hospitals, and high case-load CHCs/SDHs.
Benefits: Improved infrastructure, training for healthcare providers, standard protocols, respectful maternity care.
Implementation: Through National Health Mission at identified healthcare facilities.
More Info:https://nhm.gov.in/index1.php?lang=1&level=3&sublinkid=1307&lid=690

5. Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan (PMSMA)

Benefit: Free antenatal check-ups by gynecologists/doctors on the 9th of every month at designated government facilities.
Eligibility: All pregnant women in 2nd and 3rd trimesters.
Services: Quality ANC including screening for high-risk pregnancies, early detection of complications, free ultrasound, blood and urine tests.
Application: No formal application; visit designated government health facilities on the 9th of each month.
More Info:https://pmsma.mohfw.gov.in/

6. Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK)

Coverage: Child health screening and early intervention services for children from birth to 18 years.
Eligibility: All children aged 0-18 years in rural areas and urban slums.
Services: Screening for 4Ds - Defects at birth, Diseases, Deficiencies, Development delays including disabilities; free treatment for 30 identified health conditions.
Implementation: Through dedicated mobile health teams visiting schools and anganwadis regularly.
More Info:https://rbsk.mohfw.gov.in/RBSK/Login/Login.aspx
Disability Support Schemes

1. Assistance to Disabled Persons for Purchase/Fitting of Aids and Appliances (ADIP)

Benefit: Assistive aids and appliances to persons with disabilities at subsidized rates.
Eligibility: 40% disability or more, monthly income below ₹20,000 (varies by income bracket).
Coverage: Devices worth up to ₹10,000 free for income below ₹15,000/month; subsidized rates for others.
Application: Apply at implementing agencies (NGOs/National Institutes) with disability certificate, income proof, and Aadhaar.
More Info:https://depwd.gov.in/adip/

2. Deendayal Disabled Rehabilitation Scheme (DDRS)

Benefit: Financial assistance to NGOs for providing education, vocational training, and rehabilitation of persons with disabilities.
Services: Special schools, vocational training centers, rehabilitation centers, community-based rehabilitation programs, early intervention.
Coverage: 90% of project cost funded by the government.
Access: Services available through registered NGOs implementing the scheme.
More Info:https://depwd.gov.in/ddrs/

3. Unique Disability ID (UDID) Project

Purpose: Create a national database of persons with disabilities and issue unique ID cards.
Benefits: Online disability certificate, tracking benefits across schemes, simplified procedures.
Eligibility: All persons with benchmark disabilities (40% or more).
Application: Apply online at www.swavlambancard.gov.in with Aadhaar, photographs, existing disability certificate, etc.
More Info:https://www.swavlambancard.gov.in/

4. Scheme of National Awards for Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities

Benefit: Cash awards ranging from ₹1 lakh to ₹10 lakh, citations, and certificates.
Categories: Individual excellence, best employers, placement agencies, technological innovation, barrier-free creation, etc.
Selection: Through national selection committees.
Application: Nominations submitted to Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment by specified dates.
More Info:https://depwd.gov.in/year-wise-national-awards/

5. Inclusive Education for Disabled at Secondary Stage (IEDSS)

Benefit: Enables students with disabilities to complete secondary education in an inclusive environment.
Eligibility: Students with disabilities aged 14+ in classes IX to XII.
Provisions: ₹3,000/year/student for books and materials, ₹10,000/school for equipments, removal of architectural barriers, resource teachers.
Implementation: Through State Education Departments in government and government-aided schools.
More Info:https://www.education.gov.in/iedss

6. Disability Pension Schemes

National: Indira Gandhi National Disability Pension Scheme provides ₹300-₹500/month to BPL persons with severe disabilities.
State Schemes: Delhi (₹2,500/month), Tamil Nadu (₹1,000-₹2,000/month), Kerala (₹1,600/month), etc.
Eligibility: Usually 80% disability for national scheme; varies for state schemes (40-80%).
Application: Through local Social Welfare Departments with disability certificate, income proof, and ID.
More Info:https://www.myscheme.gov.in/schemes/igndps
Chronic Disease Management Programs

1. National Programme for Prevention and Control of Cancer, Diabetes, Cardiovascular Diseases & Stroke (NPCDCS)

Services: Screening, diagnosis, treatment for non-communicable diseases.
Facilities: NCD clinics at District and CHC levels, cardiac care units at District hospitals.
Coverage: Free diagnostics, subsidized treatment, follow-up care.
Access: Visit nearest NCD clinic or health center for screening.
More Info:https://nhm.gov.in/index1.php?lang=1&level=2&sublinkid=1048&lid=604

2. National Mental Health Programme (NMHP)

Services: Treatment and rehabilitation services for mental disorders.
Facilities: District Mental Health Program (DMHP) in 704 districts.
Components: OPD services, basic psychiatric care, counseling, follow-up, IEC activities.
Access: Available at district hospitals and some CHCs with DMHP.
More Info:https://mohfw.gov.in

3. National Tobacco Control Programme (NTCP)

Services: Tobacco cessation services, enforcement of tobacco control laws.
Facilities: Tobacco Cessation Centers at district hospitals.
Benefits: Free counseling, nicotine replacement therapy where available.
Access: Visit district hospital tobacco cessation center or call national quitline 1800-11-2356.
More Info:https://ntcp.mohfw.gov.in/

4. National Programme for Health Care of the Elderly (NPHCE)

Services: Dedicated healthcare for elderly with chronic conditions.
Facilities: Geriatric clinics at district hospitals, 10-bedded geriatric wards.
Benefits: Free specialized care, physiotherapy, rehabilitation services.
Eligibility: Individuals aged 60 years and above.
More Info:https://mohfw.gov.in

5. National Programme for Prevention and Control of Fluorosis (NPPCF)

Services: Screening, diagnosis, management of fluorosis.
Areas: 157 districts across 18 states with high fluoride levels.
Components: Calcium, vitamin D, nutrition supplements, defluoridation of water.
Access: Through district hospitals in endemic areas.
More Info:https://dghs.mohfw.gov.in

6. Revised National TB Control Programme (RNTCP) / National TB Elimination Programme (NTEP)

Services: Free diagnosis and treatment for tuberculosis.
Benefits: Free molecular testing, drugs, nutritional support (₹500/month).
Coverage: All TB patients registered under the program.
Access: Through designated microscopy centers, TB units, DOTS centers.
Prescription & Treatment Cost Support

1. Pradhan Mantri Bhartiya Janaushadhi Pariyojana (PMBJP)

Benefit: Generic medicines at affordable prices (50-90% less than market rates).
Accessibility: Over 8,800 Jan Aushadhi Kendras across India.
Coverage: Over 1,800 medicines and 285 surgical items.
Eligibility: Open to all; no eligibility criteria.
More Info:https://janaushadhi.gov.in/pmbjb-scheme

2. Health Minister's Discretionary Grant

Benefit: Financial assistance up to ₹1.25 lakh for patients below poverty line.
Eligibility: BPL patients suffering from major life-threatening diseases.
Diseases Covered: Cancer, cardiac diseases, kidney failure requiring dialysis/transplant.
Application: Apply to Ministry of Health & Family Welfare with medical certificate, hospital cost estimate, income proof, BPL certificate.
More Info:https://www.mohfw.gov.in

3. Rashtriya Arogya Nidhi (RAN)

Benefit: Financial assistance up to ₹15 lakh for BPL patients.
Eligibility: BPL patients suffering from life-threatening diseases requiring hospitalization.
Covered Hospitals: Super-specialty government hospitals like AIIMS, PGI, NIMHANS, etc.
Application: Apply through Medical Superintendent of the hospital with required documents.

4. Health Research Scheme

Benefit: Financial assistance for patients participating in treatment trials.
Eligibility: Patients with specified conditions under research at ICMR institutes.
Coverage: Free treatment, diagnostic tests, and sometimes transportation costs.
Application: Through ICMR institutes and centers conducting clinical trials.

5. State Illness Assistance Funds

Examples: Tamil Nadu Chief Minister's Comprehensive Health Insurance, Delhi Arogya Kosh, Kerala Karunya Benevolent Fund.
Benefits: Financial assistance varying from ₹50,000 to ₹5 lakh depending on state.
Eligibility: Usually for BPL families or those with annual income below specified limit.
Application: Through respective state health departments or district hospitals.

6. CGHS Wellness Centers & Free Medicines

Benefit: Free medicines and treatments at CGHS Wellness Centers.
Eligibility: CGHS beneficiaries (central govt employees, pensioners, and dependents).
Coverage: OPD consultation, medicines, tests, specialist referrals.
Access: Through 329 CGHS Wellness Centers across 75 cities in India.
Health Insurance Schemes

1. Ayushman Bharat Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (AB PM-JAY)

Coverage: Health insurance coverage of ₹5 lakh per family per year for secondary and tertiary care hospitalization.
Eligibility: Poor and vulnerable families identified based on SECC database; approximately 10.74 crore families (50 crore people).
Benefits: Covers 3 days of pre-hospitalization and 15 days of post-hospitalization expenses, 1,400+ procedures, all pre-existing conditions.
Application: PMJAY Golden Card can be obtained at PM-JAY empaneled hospitals or Common Service Centers with Aadhaar card, ration card, or other specified ID.
More Info:https://beneficiary.nha.gov.in/

2. Central Government Health Scheme (CGHS)

Coverage: Comprehensive medical care to central government employees, pensioners, and their dependents.
Eligibility: All central government employees, pensioners, members of Parliament, ex-MPs, freedom fighters, and their dependent family members.
Benefits: OPD consultation, medicines, lab tests, imaging, hospitalization, specialized treatments at empaneled private hospitals.
Application: Submit application through parent department with prescribed forms, ID proof, and photos.
More Info:https://cghs.gov.in/CghsGovIn/faces/ViewPage.xhtml

3. Employees' State Insurance Scheme (ESIS)

Coverage: Self-financing health insurance and social security scheme for Indian workers.
Eligibility: Employees earning up to ₹21,000 per month in organizations with 10+ employees (20+ in some states).
Benefits: Medical, sickness, maternity, disability, and dependent benefits; super-specialty treatment.
Application: Employer registers employees; employee contribution is 0.75% of wages, employer contributes 3.25%.
More Info:https://www.esic.gov.in/

4. Rashtriya Swasthya Bima Yojana (RSBY)

Coverage: Health insurance coverage up to ₹30,000 for BPL families (now largely merged with PM-JAY).
Eligibility: BPL families, MGNREGA workers, domestic workers, sanitation workers, rickshaw pullers, rag pickers.
Benefits: Cashless hospitalization in public and private hospitals, maternity benefits, transport allowance.
Application: Biometric smart card issued through enrollment camps in villages/urban areas.
More Info:https://www.india.gov.in/spotlight/rashtriya-swasthya-bima-yojana

5. Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana (PMSBY)

Coverage: Accident insurance with coverage of ₹2 lakh for accidental death or disability.
Eligibility: Indian citizens aged 18-70 years with a bank account.
Benefits: ₹2 lakh for accidental death/permanent disability, ₹1 lakh for partial disability.
Application: Annual premium of ₹12 auto-debited from bank account; apply through bank or online.
More Info:https://financialservices.gov.in/beta/en/pmsby

6. State Government Health Insurance Schemes

Examples: Tamil Nadu's Chief Minister's Comprehensive Health Insurance Scheme, Kerala's Karunya Arogya Suraksha Padhathi (KASP), Karnataka's Arogya Bhagya, etc.
Eligibility: Varies by state, typically covering BPL families and specific vulnerable groups.
Benefits: Coverage ranges from ₹1.5 lakh to ₹5 lakh depending on the state.
Application: Through state health departments, district hospitals, or specified enrollment centers.
More Info:https://beneficiary.nha.gov.in/
Emergency & Critical Care Support

1. National Ambulance Service (108)

Services: Free emergency ambulance service for medical, police, and fire emergencies.
